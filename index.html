The Digital Oral History and Transcript Analysis Platform is designed to present and analyze a selection of oral history interviews through an intuitive and visually engaging website. With a layout modeled on a clean interactive research environment, the site hosts readable, annotated transcripts and audio files enhanced by modern natural language processing tools. These include keyword frequency tracking, and named entity recognition, helping users explore themes, patterns, and significant terms that emerge organically from the interview content.

This project leverages general corpus linguistic methods alongside manual interpretation to bridge the gap between machine analysis and human understanding. Through integration with transcription tools such as OpenAI Whisper and PDF parsing capabilities, the platform automates large portions of data processing while offering editorial oversight. The platform serves as both a research tool and public history resource. The user is encouraged to explore both the technical infrastructure of the transcripts and the thematic core of the project, using linked visual tools, graphs, and text analysis widgets to build their own interpretations of the oral history materials.
